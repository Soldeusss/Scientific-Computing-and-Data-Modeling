\section*{Introduction}

The purpose of this lab is to measure the resistance,$R$,of three resistors.This was done by connecting the resistors, with a negative and positve lead, to a volmeter.Then we connected the voltmeter with only a positve lead to a bench powersupply which would provide power and a way to measure the current. We would then slowly increase the current and record the value of both the current and voltage. We repeated this experiment until we had 10 measurements for current and voltage for all three resistors. The result of the experiment is as follows; for resistor one $ R1=976.85$ $\pm$ $3.96$ $\Omega$ , for resistor two $ R2=3266.05$ $\pm$ $99.4$ $\Omega$ and for resistor three $ R2=1489.59$ $\pm$ $60.684$ $\Omega$

The fundemental equation for Ohm's Law is used for this lab:
% e1
\begin{equation}
V=IR
\end{equation}
In this equation, the voltage $V$, is equal to $I$, the current, times the resistance $R$ of the material. Both $I$ and $V$ was the recorded data for this lab. We can then rearrange the Ohm's law equation to solve for resistance. 

% e2
\begin{equation}
S=R
\end{equation}
Rearranging Ohm's law to solve for resistance gives us Equation (2), which is in the form of the equation of a line in the form of $slope=R$.

\section*{Methods}

A positive and a negative lead was connected to both sides of a resistor. Next the leads are connected to a voltmeter. Then we connected the voltmeter using a positive lead to a bench power supply. The current was set to a direct current. Next we moved the knobs until we had a stable and consistant current .Then we increased the current and took measurements of the voltage and current. This process was repeated until we 10 data points for both $I$ and $V$ for all three resistors.

The value of $slope$ is determined from a least squares fit to $I$ plotted as a function of $V$. The value of $R$ is calculated using $I$ and $V$. The uncertainty in $R$ is calculated by propagating the uncertainty for $S$  in the calculation for $R$. Unless otherwise noted, all uncertainties are reported using $t$-statistics for 95\% confidence intervals and confidence bands.  All calculations are performed in MATLAB\footnote{The Mathworks, 
http://www.mathworks.com}.
